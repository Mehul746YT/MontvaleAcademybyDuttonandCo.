Business Administration

Montvale Academy Assessment

📖 About This Assessment

This assessment evaluates your understanding of business administration concepts, including management, finance, marketing, operations, and strategic decision-making.

Candidates are expected to prepare independently using books, courses, and other educational resources before attempting the examination.

📚 Study Material & Recommended Reading

Before attempting the assessment, we strongly recommend reviewing the following topics and resources. Thorough preparation is essential — the pass mark is 85%.

Strategic Management

  • Porter's Generic Strategies — Cost Leadership, Differentiation, and Focus. Understand when and how each is applied.
  • Mergers & Acquisitions — The concept of synergy, hostile takeovers, and due diligence processes.
  • Balanced Scorecard — The four perspectives: Financial, Customer, Internal Process, and Learning & Growth.
  • Fiduciary Duty — Legal obligations of corporate officers to shareholders and stakeholders.

Financial Analysis

  • The Matching Principle — Why expenses must be recorded in the same period as the revenue they generate.
  • Net Working Capital — Current Assets minus Current Liabilities; a measure of short-term financial health.
  • Quick Ratio (Acid Test) — Measures short-term liquidity excluding inventory.
  • Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) — Calculating expected return based on systematic risk (beta).
  • Rule of 72 — A quick method to estimate how long it takes for an investment to double.

Operations & Efficiency

  • Lean Manufacturing — Eliminating waste (muda) to maximise value for customers.
  • Six Sigma (DMAIC) — Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control methodology for process improvement.
  • Theory of Constraints — Identifying and managing the system's bottleneck to improve throughput.
  • Economies of Scale — How average unit costs decrease as production volume increases.
  • Transfer Pricing — Pricing for internal transactions between divisions of the same company.

Organisational Behaviour

  • Agency Problem — The conflict between shareholders' and management's interests.
  • Matrix Structure — Advantages and challenges of dual reporting lines.
  • Golden Parachute — Executive compensation packages triggered by change of control.
  • Transformational vs. Transactional Leadership — Inspiration and change vs. reward and compliance.

Market Dynamics

  • Perfect Competition — Why firms are "price takers" with many buyers/sellers and perfect information.
  • Price Elasticity of Demand — How responsive quantity demanded is to a change in price.
  • Leading vs. Lagging Indicators — Consumer Confidence Index (leading) vs. GDP and unemployment (lagging).
  • Operating Leverage — The relationship between fixed costs and profit sensitivity to revenue changes.
  • Product Life Cycle — Introduction, Growth, Maturity, and Decline stages.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Principles of Management by Peter Drucker
  • Competitive Strategy by Michael E. Porter
  • Financial Intelligence by Karen Berman & Joe Knight
  • The Lean Startup by Eric Ries
  • Organisational Behaviour by Stephen P. Robbins
